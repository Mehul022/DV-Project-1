Nailympia Overview

What is Nailympia?

Nailympia is a global nail competition founded in 2001, providing a non-product biased platform for artists. Events are held worldwide, including in London, Rio, DÃ¼sseldorf, and the USA.

Summary of the Event and Dataset

Nailympia brings together top nail artists from around the world, competing in various categories such as Competitive, Salon, and Stiletto. The event showcases creativity, technique, and innovation in the nail industry.

The dataset includes details on judges, winners by event, and a comparison of nail categories. Judges' data highlights their experience, specialty, and contributions to competition standards. The winners' dataset captures event divisions, geographic spread, and year-wise competition insights. The category dataset compares judging criteria among different styles.

Questions and Purposes

1. How often do countries with fewer judges participate in international events compared to countries with a larger representation and does more judges lead to a higher number of winners?

Purpose: To analyze the correlation between the number of judges from a country and the number of winners from that country.

2. How do the judging priorities differ among Competitive, Salon, and Stiletto categories?

Purpose: To understand the different judging criteria and their importance across various competition categories.

3. Do judges with certain expertise tend to have judged more number of events on average and have remained judge for more number of years on average in comparison to those with other types of expertise?

Purpose: To analyze the experience and event participation of judges based on their areas of expertise.
